Meet Jane Doe

Learn more about the members who make our union strong.

Jane Doe

Office Specialist II

College of Engineering

"Our union gives us a collective voice that we wouldn't have individually. It’s about ensuring everyone is treated with fairness and respect."

How long have you worked at OSU and what do you do?

I’ve been at Oregon State for 12 years now. As an Office Specialist in the College of Engineering, I support students and faculty with administrative tasks, scheduling, and making sure the department runs smoothly day-to-day. No two days are ever the same, which keeps things interesting!

Why did you decide to become a union member?

When I first started, I wasn't very familiar with unions. But I saw how experienced members supported a coworker through a difficult situation with management. It made me realize that the union isn't some outside group; it's us, looking out for each other. I joined because I believe we are all stronger when we stand together.

What does solidarity mean to you?

To me, solidarity is about empathy and action. It’s understanding that an issue affecting a custodian or a food service worker is my issue, too, because we are all part of the same community. It’s showing up—by wearing purple on Wednesdays, attending a meeting, or just listening to a coworker—to show that you care and that you have their back.

What would you say to a new employee who is on the fence about joining?

I would tell them that being a member is the best way to have a real say in your workplace. The contract we all work under doesn't just appear—it's negotiated by us, for us. By becoming a member, you get to vote on that contract, elect your representatives, and be part of the decisions that shape our jobs and our lives at OSU.
