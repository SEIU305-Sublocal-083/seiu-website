Union Basics

What Is a CAT?

The Contract Action Team is how members keep each other informed, build participation, and turn bargaining priorities into visible workplace power.

Short answer: A CAT is a group of members who help coworkers understand what is happening in bargaining and take action together.

What CAT members do

  • Share bargaining updates with coworkers in plain language.
  • Listen for workplace concerns and bring patterns back to union leaders.
  • Invite coworkers to meetings, rallies, purple-up days, and bargaining actions.
  • Help make sure bargaining is not just something happening at the table, but something workers are visibly backing across campus.

Why CATs matter during bargaining

Management pays attention to whether workers are organized. A strong CAT helps show that our bargaining team has real member support behind them.

CAT work is also practical. Members are more likely to participate when a coworker they know explains what is at stake and how to plug in.

How to get involved

You do not need to be an expert to start. Come to a CAT meeting, ask for the latest bargaining materials, and commit to talking with a few coworkers in your area.
