LEGISLATIVE ADVOCACY

SEIU Leadership Meets with State Legislators in Salem

By SEIU 503 Sublocal 083, Union Leadership

Last updated: February 6, 2026

Created: 2026-02-06

Published: 2026-02-06

Salem, OR - SEIU Local 503 leaders met with state legislators this week to push for policy and budget priorities that directly affect working families and public services across Oregon.

The delegation shared member stories from campuses and public agencies, focusing on staffing shortages, rising costs, and workload pressures that continue to strain workers and the services communities depend on.

Key priorities discussed

  • State investments to fill critical vacancies and stabilize frontline staffing.
  • Strong cost-of-living support to protect worker pay from inflation.
  • Enforcement and accountability for workplace safety protections.
  • Long-term training pathways that recruit and retain Oregon workers.
"When workers are stretched thin, students and communities feel it. We are calling on lawmakers to invest in the people who keep Oregon running." - SEIU 503 Sublocal 083 Leadership Team

Legislators signaled continued engagement as budget and labor policy proposals move through committee. SEIU leaders will remain in contact with offices in Salem to advance these priorities and keep members informed of next steps.
