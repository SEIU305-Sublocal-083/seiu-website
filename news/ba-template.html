BARGAINING UPDATE

Tentative Agreement Reached!

Published on August 1, 2025

Corvallis, OR — After months of intense negotiations, your SEIU 503 bargaining team is thrilled to announce that we have reached a Tentative Agreement (TA) with Oregon State University management on a new two-year contract. This agreement represents a significant victory and is a direct result of the incredible solidarity shown by members like you across campus.

From wearing purple on Wednesdays to attending rallies and talking with your coworkers, your actions sent a powerful message to management that we were united in our fight for a fair contract. This TA would not have been possible without you.

Highlights of the Agreement

While the full text will be available soon, here are some of the key wins we secured in this Tentative Agreement:

  • Significant Wage Increases: A guaranteed cost-of-living adjustment (COLA) for all members in both years of the contract, plus additional step increases.
  • Healthcare Protections: We successfully fought back against proposals that would have increased healthcare costs for members and their families.
  • Improved Safety Language: New contract language that strengthens workplace safety protocols and gives members a stronger voice in safety committees.
  • Fair Workload Protections: Clearer processes for addressing and resolving workload issues to prevent burnout.
"This agreement is a testament to what we can achieve when we stand together. It's a win for every classified employee at OSU and a major step toward a more just and equitable campus." — Russ Born, President

What Happens Next? Ratification Vote!

A Tentative Agreement means our bargaining team is recommending this contract for approval, but it is not final until members vote to accept it. All SEIU 503 members will have the opportunity to vote on whether to ratify this new contract.

Information sessions will be held next week to review the TA in detail and answer any questions you may have. Details on the sessions and the electronic voting process will be sent out via email shortly. Please make sure your contact information is up to date to ensure you receive your ballot.
