What Is a Steward?

A steward is a trained coworker who helps members understand the contract, exercise their rights, and respond when workplace problems come up.

By SEIU 503 Sublocal 083, Union Leadership

Published June 10, 2026

A union steward is a coworker who has been trained to represent members and help enforce our contract. Stewards are part of our local union structure, and they are often the first person members should contact when something at work does not feel right.

Stewards are not outside officials. They are classified workers at OSU who volunteer their time because they believe every member deserves support, information, and a fair process.

What stewards do

Stewards help members move from uncertainty to action. Sometimes that means answering a quick contract question. Sometimes it means helping document a problem, preparing for a meeting, or filing a grievance.

  • Answer contract questions. Stewards help members understand what the collective bargaining agreement says and how it applies at work.
  • Represent members. If you are called into an investigatory meeting that could lead to discipline, you have the right to ask for a steward under your Weingarten Rights.
  • Support grievances. When management violates the contract, stewards can help members identify the issue, gather documentation, and move through the grievance process.
  • Track patterns. Stewards help our union see when problems are isolated and when they point to a broader workplace issue.
  • Connect members with resources. Stewards can point members toward union leadership, rights resources, and other support when an issue needs more help.

When should you contact a steward?

Reach out early. You do not need to wait until a situation becomes formal or disciplinary. If you have a question about pay, scheduling, leave, workload, discipline, supervision, classification, safety, or any workplace concern, a steward can help you think through next steps.

If management asks questions that could lead to discipline, clearly say that you want a union steward present before the meeting continues. Then contact the steward team as soon as you can.

How is a steward different from a CAT member?

Stewards focus on representation and contract enforcement. CAT members focus on organizing and communication during bargaining and workplace actions. Both roles matter. A strong union needs trained stewards who defend rights and member organizers who keep coworkers connected.
