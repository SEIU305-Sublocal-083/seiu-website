Union Basics

What Is a Union Steward?

A steward is a trained coworker who helps members understand the contract, exercise their rights, and respond when workplace problems come up.

Short answer: A steward is a coworker you can contact when you need union representation, contract guidance, or help figuring out next steps at work.

What stewards do

  • Help members understand contract rights and workplace processes.
  • Represent members in investigatory meetings and other situations where discipline may be possible.
  • Help document issues, ask questions, and connect members with the right union resources.
  • Bring workplace patterns back to union leadership so problems are not treated as isolated incidents.

When to contact a steward

Contact a steward when management calls you into a meeting that could lead to discipline, when ELR contacts you, when a workplace problem keeps repeating, or when you are not sure whether the contract applies.

What to say if you need representation

If a meeting could lead to discipline, you can say: I request union representation. I refuse to answer questions until my union representative is present.
