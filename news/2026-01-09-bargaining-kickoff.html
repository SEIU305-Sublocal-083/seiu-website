BARGAINING KICKOFF

2026 Bargaining Kickoff: Why This Year Matters

By SEIU 503 Sublocal 083 Union Leadership

Last updated: January 9, 2026

Created: 2026-01-09

Published: 2026-01-09

Local Roots, Collective Power

Corvallis, OR — This isn't just about a contract; it's about our livelihood and the future of our work at OSU. As we enter 2026, we face a critical opportunity to define what "fairness" means for classified staff at Oregon State University.

For over 150 years, classified staff have been the backbone of Oregon State University. From the maintenance of historic buildings to the administrative work that powers modern research, our labor has built this institution. Yet, today we find ourselves fighting for basic economic dignity.

New Year, New Contract Fight

The results from our recent "Fighting for Higher Ed" survey were loud and clear. With record-high participation, members across every department signaled that the status quo is no longer acceptable. You told us that stagnant wages in the face of rising living costs are unsustainable.

Our bargaining team has heard you. Based on your feedback, we have set a clear agenda for the upcoming negotiations:

  • Real COLA: We are fighting for a Cost of Living Adjustment that actually keeps up with inflation.
  • Benefit Protections: Defending our healthcare and retirement from any proposed cuts.
  • Workload Equity: Addressing the increasing demands placed on staff without adequate support.
  • Rights Enforcement: Strengthening our ability to enforce Weingarten Rights and safety protocols.
"We are proud public employees, not just 'staff.' This year, we fight to ensure the university recognizes the value we bring every single day." — SEIU 503 Bargaining Team

Mobilize: We Need You

A strong contract starts with a strong union. Management is watching. If we stay silent, they will assume we are satisfied. We cannot win this at the bargaining table alone; we need every member to stand with us.
