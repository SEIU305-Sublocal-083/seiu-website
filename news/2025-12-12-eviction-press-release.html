FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE: Oregon State University Unlawfully Evicts Union from Adams Hall Office

Published on December 12, 2025

Corvallis, OR â€” SEIU 503 Sublocal 083, the union representing classified staff at Oregon State University, has been unlawfully evicted from its long-held office in Adams Hall. The university administration's actions represent a blatant disregard for the rights of its employees and a direct attack on the union's ability to represent its members.

The union office in Adams Hall has served as a critical hub for members to seek assistance, access resources, and organize for better working conditions. By evicting the union, OSU is attempting to silence the voices of its most essential employees and undermine their collective power.

A History of Disrespect

This eviction is the latest in a series of escalating actions by the OSU administration that demonstrate a profound lack of respect for its classified staff. The union has repeatedly attempted to engage with the university in good faith, only to be met with resistance, delays, and now, this hostile and unlawful act.

"This is a direct attack on our union and our ability to advocate for our members. We will not be silenced, and we will not be intimidated. We are exploring all legal options to fight this unlawful eviction."

A Call to Action

SEIU 503 Sublocal 083 is calling on all members and allies to stand in solidarity against this injustice. The union will be holding a day of action to protest the eviction and demand that OSU restore access to the Adams Hall office.
