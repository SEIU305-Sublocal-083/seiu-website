What to include in your first message

You do not need to identify the correct contract article or solve the problem before reaching out. A short, factual summary is enough to begin.

Protect sensitive information: Do not include Social Security numbers, passwords or unnecessary medical details in an initial email. You may use a personal email address if you prefer to keep correspondence outside university systems.
