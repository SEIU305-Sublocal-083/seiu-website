OCTOBER NEWSLETTER

Website Launch, Bowling Strikes, & Big Wins!

Published on October 2, 2025

Hello Siblings,

For those I haven't had the pleasure of meeting yet, my name is Jax Johnson, and I’m honored to be our Communications Chair. I'm incredibly excited to be in this role, especially with all the great things we have on the horizon. There's a big project I've been pouring my energy into that's finally ready, a chance for us to get together and knock down some pins, and a major victory for our union family to celebrate. So, let’s get right to the good stuff.

Behold! The Website Has Arrived!

It's ALIVE! After countless hours of wrangling code, chasing pixels, and imbibing truly heroic amounts of coffee, our digital home is officially open for business. I am incredibly proud to launch www.local083.org! This website is built for you – to be a one-stop shop for your contract, event info, news, and resources. A massive, heartfelt thank you to our entire executive team and the nascent Comms Committee who empowered me and provided the support to make this happen. You all rock.

Now that we have this great new tool, it's time to use it to get together. Which brings me to...

Let’s Go Bowling!

That’s right, it’s time to trade our work shoes for something a little more… slippery. Join us for our Member & Family Bowling Night! It’s all going down on Thursday, October 23rd, from 6:00 PM to 8:00 PM at Lanes & Games in the MU Basement. We’ll cover the food, lanes, and shoes. You just bring your amazing self, your family, and your questionable bowling form. It's less about strikes and more about solidarity (and maybe a little friendly trash talk). Let's pack the lanes!

RSVP for Bowling Night!

Building community is what our union is all about, and that spirit of solidarity is how we win real victories for workers. Speaking of victories...

Solidarity in Action: A HUGE Win for ODOT!

Let's give a massive round of applause to our union siblings in the Oregon Department of Transportation! After months of sleepless nights and difficult conversations, their powerful organizing and unwavering solidarity have paid off. They successfully fought back against planned layoffs, which have now been reversed thanks to a new funding package passed in Salem.

This is a monumental victory and a perfect example of what happens when we stand together and advocate for ourselves. This fight isn't just about jobs; it's about the essential services we provide to Oregonians every day. As SEIU 503 Executive Director Melissa Unger said:

"...legislators voted for this package because of you, because they know the important work you do to keep our communities and our roads safe."

— Melissa Unger, SEIU 503 Executive Director

Their fight is our fight, and their victory is a win for all of us in the labor movement. POWERFUL!

Important Reminders

Get Ready to Run! Elections are coming up for all leadership positions in our local. Nominations open on December 1, 2025, and close on January 23, 2026. All leadership positions are open for election, including President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, Chief Steward, Membership Coordinator, General Council Delegate (5), and Bargaining Delegate. If you've ever thought about getting more involved, this is a great opportunity!

Timesheet Reminder: Just a friendly reminder that timesheets are due on the 15th of every month. Make sure to get yours in on time!

Bargaining is Coming: COLA & Survey Updates

As we celebrate these wins, we're also gearing up for our own contract negotiations. A quick reminder that our next Cost-of-Living Adjustment (COLA) of 3.5% will take effect this coming January. This is a direct result of our last contract fight, and it's a crucial part of keeping our wages in line with inflation.

To prepare for the next round of bargaining, we need to hear from you! Our bargaining survey is the most important tool we have to understand what matters most to our members. So far, over 300 of you have filled it out – a fantastic start! But we want to hear from every single member. If you haven't yet, please take 10 minutes to make your voice heard. The more responses we have, the stronger our position will be at the bargaining table.

Your Union Needs YOU!

Feeling inspired? Good! That victory was won by members getting involved, and you can do the same. Here are a couple of key ways to get more involved right now:

  • Come to Bowling Night! Seriously, just showing up and connecting with your fellow members is a powerful act of solidarity.
  • Become a CAT! Our Contract Action Team (CAT) is the eyes and ears of our union in every workplace. You'll be a key communicator, helping to share info and organize your coworkers. It’s a vital role as we head toward bargaining.
  • Got a Workplace Concern? Don't go it alone. If something doesn't feel right at work, your first step should be to reach out to a steward. They're trained to help you understand your rights and navigate workplace issues.

That's all for now, folks. Go check out the new site, sign up for bowling, and let’s keep building our power together.

In Solidarity (and occasional absurdity),

Jax SN Johnson

Communications Chair, SEIU 503 Sub Local 083
