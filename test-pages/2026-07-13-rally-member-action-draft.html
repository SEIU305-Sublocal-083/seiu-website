MEMBER ACTION DRAFT · NOT PUBLISHED 2026 BARGAINING

We brought the noise. Now we build the power.

More than 140 of us filled McNary Field and marched to Kerr. Management saw our strength. What happens next depends on all of us.

By SEIU Local 503 Sublocal 083

Published July 13, 2026

More than 140 members, families and supporters showed up June 30—and Oregon State University could not look away.

We filled McNary Field. We marched to Kerr Administration. We chanted for dignity, respect and more money in our checks on the same day our contract expired.

That rally proved something important: bargaining power does not come only from the people sitting at the table. It comes from every worker willing to stand with a coworker and say, “We deserve better.”

“Our rally at OSU fired me up. It once again showed that bargaining doesn't just happen at the table, it happens in the field, too, and we brought the noise. I couldn't be more proud and honored to be in this fight with our members.”

— Damien Manassa, OSU bargaining delegate

We showed what solidarity looks like

Workers came with coworkers. Families came together. Community supporters and state Rep. Sarah Finger McDonald stood with us. Across McNary Field, members answered one another: “We fight, we win.”

That energy matters—but only if we carry it forward. Management is counting on workers getting tired, tuning out or waiting for someone else to act. Our answer is more conversations, more participation and more members ready for the next step.

The rally was not the finish line

June 30 was a warning: classified staff are watching, organizing and ready to fight for a contract that reflects the value of our work.

Now every member has a role. Talk with a coworker. Share the bargaining update. Join the Contract Action Team. Show management that the crowd at McNary Field was only the beginning.
