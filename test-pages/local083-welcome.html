Your rights in 60 seconds

If a meeting could lead to discipline, you have the right to union representation. Use this sentence to invoke your rights:

I request union representation. I refuse to answer questions until my union representative is present.

You can pause any investigatory interview.
A steward can advise you and ask clarifying questions.
Management cannot discipline you for invoking this right.

What is a union?

A union is an organization of you and your coworkers, united to make changes you cannot do alone. We work together for better pay, benefits, and working conditions, and we hold management accountable to fair treatment.

Our scale in Oregon

SEIU represents 72,000 workers across Oregon, including 25,000 state workers.

Political power matters

Citizen’s Action for Political Education (CAPE) is a political action fund. It is an optional additional contribution that helps build power. Contributing members interview candidates and decide on endorsements. Political decisions directly impact your pay and working conditions.

Quick answers

Am I covered by the contract?

Yes. All classified staff in the bargaining unit are covered. Members also vote on contracts and elect union leadership.

How do I reach a steward?

Email 083stewards@seiu503.org and a steward will connect with you.

What does membership change?

Membership gives you a vote, a voice, and the ability to help set bargaining priorities.

Where can I learn more?

Start with the Resources & Rights page, then check Events for meetings and trainings.

Become a member
