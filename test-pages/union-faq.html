Union FAQ

A quick guide to how Local 083 works and how membership strengthens your workplace.

What is a union?

A union is an organization of you and your coworkers, united to make changes you cannot do alone. We work together for better pay, benefits, and working conditions, and we hold management accountable to fair treatment.

Scale in Oregon

SEIU represents 72,000 workers across Oregon, including 25,000 state workers.

Collective power

When we unite, we have the power to protect each other and improve our workplace.

Common questions

Am I covered by the contract?

Yes. All classified staff in the bargaining unit are covered. Members also vote on contracts and elect leadership.

What does membership change?

Membership gives you a vote, a voice, and the ability to help set bargaining priorities.

How do I reach a steward?

Email 083stewards@seiu503.org and a steward will connect with you.

Where do I start?

Read the contract and your rights, then attend a membership meeting to meet coworkers.

Why members win

Higher wages

On average, union workers’ wages are 19% higher than nonunion workers.

Better benefits

96% of union workers have employer-provided health insurance compared to 69% of nonunion workers.
