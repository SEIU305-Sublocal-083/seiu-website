Strike Pledge Drive and Bargaining Q&A

Join Johnny Earl and Damien Manassa in MU 211 or on Zoom for a conversation about bargaining and a chance to sign the Higher Ed strike pledge.

Bring your questions

Join in person or on Zoom for a clear update on bargaining and straightforward answers about what a possible strike would mean.

SEIU Local 503 President Johnny Earl will join us as a special guest. OSU bargaining delegate Damien Manassa will share where bargaining stands and what our team needs from members and coworkers now.

What we’ll discuss

  • Where Higher Ed bargaining stands and what remains unresolved.
  • What a strike is and how the process works.
  • What the strike pledge means—and what it does not mean.
  • How member participation gives our bargaining team more power.

Higher Ed works because we do

Why sign the strike pledge?

The strike pledge is not a strike-authorization vote. It tells our bargaining team and management that workers reject what is on the table and are ready to keep fighting for a fair contract.

Every signature builds our collective power. A strong pledge shows management that workers are informed, organized and standing together behind our bargaining team.
