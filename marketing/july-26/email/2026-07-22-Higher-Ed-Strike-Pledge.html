Hi everyone,

Management is still at 0% COLA. We need to show that university workers are ready to fight for more.

At Tuesday’s mediation session, management still had not moved on cost-of-living raises. Takeaways affecting layoff and bumping rights, contracting out and health care also remain on the table.

Management did move off its original position on steps after workers across all seven universities spoke out. That movement matters—and it shows why we must keep the pressure on.

What the pledge means

The strike pledge is not a strike-authorization vote. It shows our bargaining team and management that workers reject what is on the table and are prepared to keep fighting for a contract we can live with.
Sign the Higher Ed strike pledge

Build the response

Make a list of 10 coworkers. Ask each person directly to read and sign the pledge. A strong response in the first few days gives our bargaining team more power at the table.

If a coworker is not yet a member, send them to local083.org/signup.

Management is watching whether hundreds of us are frustrated or thousands of us are organized. Let’s give our bargaining team the answer it needs.

In solidarity,
SEIU Local 503 at Oregon State University
