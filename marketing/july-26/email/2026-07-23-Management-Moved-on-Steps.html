Member pressure works

We made noise. Management moved.

A crack in their position—not a fair contract yet.

Hi everyone,

Workers across Oregon’s seven public universities told management: HELL NO.

We rallied, emailed university leaders, observed bargaining and talked with coworkers. At Tuesday’s mediation session, management moved off its original position on steps and returned with a different approach.

Our bargaining team credits member action with forcing that movement. The revised direction still falls short, and our team has not accepted it. But management moved because workers made it clear that its original position would not settle this contract.

Movement is not a settlement

Management is still at 0% COLA. Takeaways affecting layoff and bumping rights, contracting out and health care remain. We can recognize movement on steps without pretending the overall offer is good enough.

The lesson is simple: when workers act together, management responds. Now we need to turn hundreds of active members into thousands.
