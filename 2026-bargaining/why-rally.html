June 30 Rally Guide

Why show up for the OSU rally on June 30?

Because a bargaining table is not only a meeting. It is a power test. When classified workers and supporters are visible together, management sees that wages, healthcare, staffing, and job protections are public university issues.

Sign Up for the Rally View Event Details

The short version

Management is offering no step increases, no COLAs, higher healthcare costs, and weaker protections. Classified staff do the daily work that keeps OSU open, clean, safe, responsive, and ready for students. The June 30 rally is a public reminder that the university cannot claim to value that work while asking workers to fall behind.

Turnout matters because it changes what decision-makers believe is possible. A quiet workforce can be ignored. A visible workforce, joined by students, faculty, researchers, families, and labor allies, is harder to dismiss.

What the rally tells management

  • Classified workers understand the offer and reject a no-raise path.
  • Support for a fair contract is broader than one work unit or one department.
  • Campus stability depends on retaining experienced classified staff.
  • Students, faculty, researchers, families, and community members notice when the people who make OSU run are squeezed.

Who should come?

If you care about public higher education, you have a reason to attend. You do not have to speak, chant, or be an expert on the contract. Showing up, wearing purple, and standing with classified workers is useful by itself.

Classified staff who are not yet members The contract affects you too. Students Staff working conditions shape student services. Faculty and researchers Research, instruction, and departments rely on staff. Families and community allies Stable campus jobs strengthen the community.

If you can only do one thing

Sign up, add the event to your calendar, and ask one person to come with you. A rally becomes powerful when turnout is organized one conversation at a time.
