For OSU Students

Student services run on classified staff labor.

From offices and labs to dining, facilities, technology, records, housing, extension, and student support, classified staff make the campus work. When staff are pushed behind economically, students feel the strain through turnover, understaffing, delays, and fewer experienced people available to help.

Sign Up for the Rally Why Turnout Matters

Why this is a student issue

Students see the university through services: clean buildings, safe spaces, working technology, housing support, food service, registration help, lab support, administrative offices, library services, and problem-solving from staff who know how OSU works. Those services depend on people staying in jobs long enough to build skill and institutional knowledge.

A no-raise offer is not separate from student life. If wages fall behind and healthcare costs rise, OSU risks more turnover, lower morale, and more pressure on the staff students rely on every day.

How students can help without overcomplicating it

  1. Sign up for the June 30 rally so organizers can plan turnout.
  2. Invite classmates, club members, roommates, and student coworkers.
  3. Share the event link with a short note about why staff work matters to your campus experience.
  4. When you talk about the rally, keep the message simple: OSU workers deserve real raises, affordable healthcare, and a fair contract.

What to say to other students

Try this: "Classified staff keep OSU running. They are bargaining against no raises, higher healthcare costs, and weaker job protections. Students should stand with them because staff working conditions are student learning conditions."

You do not need to speak for the union. You can share why a stable, experienced, respected staff workforce matters to you.
