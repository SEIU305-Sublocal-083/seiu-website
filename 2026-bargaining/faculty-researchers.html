For Faculty and Researchers

Research and instruction depend on stable classified staff jobs.

Faculty and researchers work closely with classified staff in labs, offices, field programs, student services, IT, facilities, finance, grant support, records, and operations. Supporting a fair contract is a practical way to defend the infrastructure that makes academic work possible.

Why faculty and researcher support matters

When classified staff leave because wages fall behind or healthcare costs rise, faculty and research teams lose people who understand systems, deadlines, compliance requirements, purchasing, hiring, grants, lab support, classroom logistics, building needs, and student-facing processes.

Turnover does not stay neatly inside one bargaining unit. It shows up as delays, repeated training, unresolved tickets, missed institutional memory, and more unpaid coordination work for everyone else.

What to do before June 30

  • Attend the rally if your schedule allows.
  • Invite colleagues who rely on classified staff in their departments, labs, colleges, or centers.
  • Share the event link in appropriate department channels with a simple note of support.
  • Talk about the connection between fair staff wages and stable research, instruction, and student support.

Suggested message

"Our teaching, research, and service work depends on classified staff. A no-raise offer and higher healthcare costs make it harder to retain the experienced staff OSU needs. I am supporting the June 30 rally because a fair contract supports the whole university."
