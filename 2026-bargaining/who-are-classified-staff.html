OSU Classified Staff Explainer

Who are classified staff at Oregon State University?

Classified staff are the workers who keep public universities functioning day after day. At OSU, that work stretches across student services, administrative offices, labs, housing, dining, facilities, custodial services, technology, extension, research support, and more.

Classified staff make the campus work

When a student gets help from an office, a building is cleaned and maintained, a lab has support, a payroll question is answered, a technology problem gets resolved, records are processed, a meal is served, a shipment moves, an event is set up, or a department stays organized, there is often classified staff labor behind it.

That work is sometimes less visible than teaching or executive decision-making, but it is not optional. OSU cannot deliver education, research, outreach, housing, dining, and student services without the workers who keep the systems moving.

Examples of classified staff work

  • Administrative, office, records, finance, purchasing, and program support.
  • Facilities, maintenance, trades, custodial, grounds, and building operations.
  • Dining, housing, events, student services, and front-line campus support.
  • IT, technical, lab, research, extension, and specialized operational support.
  • Work that protects continuity when students, faculty, researchers, and departments need help fast.

Why the contract matters

The contract sets wages, benefits, job protections, overtime rules, leave provisions, and core workplace rights. In 2026 bargaining, management's offer would freeze steps and COLAs, shift healthcare costs, and weaken protections. That is why classified staff are organizing publicly for a fair contract.

Fair contract standards help OSU recruit and retain skilled workers. When experienced classified staff stay, students and departments benefit from faster answers, fewer disruptions, and people who know how to navigate the university.
