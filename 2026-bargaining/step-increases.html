2026 Bargaining Issue Guide

Step increases recognize service, experience, and skill.

For classified staff, step movement is part of the wage system. Freezing steps tells workers that experience at OSU should not translate into economic progress.

Read the Bargaining HubCompare COLA Issues

Why step movement matters

Step increases create predictable progress within a classification. They help workers plan, reward experience, and reduce churn in jobs that require institutional knowledge.

  • Step movement is separate from a cost-of-living adjustment.
  • Workers can lose ground when either step movement or COLAs are frozen.
  • A long step freeze makes it harder to retain experienced classified staff.

What our union is fighting for

Our union is fighting to protect step movement and win a wage package that values the workers who keep OSU running every day.

How to talk about it

Ask coworkers what a four-year pause in wage progression would mean for rent, childcare, transportation, and staying in public service work. Those stories help show why turnout matters.
