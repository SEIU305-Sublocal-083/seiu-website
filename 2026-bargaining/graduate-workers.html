For Graduate Workers

Campus labor is stronger when workers show up for each other.

Graduate workers and classified staff experience the same university from different jobs. Showing up for classified staff on June 30 builds solidarity around a simple principle: the people who teach, research, support students, maintain buildings, run systems, and keep campus moving deserve fair treatment.

Sign Up for the Rally Why Rally

Why graduate workers have a stake

Graduate workers know what it means for a university to rely on underpaid or stretched labor. Classified staff face a management offer with no step increases, no COLAs, higher healthcare costs, and weakened protections. That is a campus-wide warning sign, not a private bargaining detail.

When one group of workers is told to absorb costs quietly, it makes that demand easier to repeat elsewhere. Solidarity helps set a different expectation: OSU should fund the work that makes education and research possible.

How to show support

  • Attend the rally if you are free and it does not conflict with your work obligations.
  • Invite labmates, cohort members, and other graduate student workers who care about campus labor.
  • Share the rally link with your own words about why worker solidarity matters.
  • Bring a sign that supports fair wages, affordable healthcare, and respect for campus workers.

Keep the message grounded

Use language that speaks for yourself unless your organization has made a formal decision. A good message is direct: "I am showing up because classified staff keep OSU running, and all campus workers benefit when management sees visible solidarity."
