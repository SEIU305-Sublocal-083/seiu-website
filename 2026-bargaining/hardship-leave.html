2026 Bargaining Issue Guide

Hardship Leave is solidarity written into the contract.

Hardship Leave lets coworkers donate leave to support a member facing a serious medical crisis. It is a practical safety net built by workers for workers.

Read the Hardship Leave GuideReturn to Bargaining Hub

Why this benefit matters

When a coworker is sick, recovering, or caring for a serious medical situation, unpaid leave can become a financial emergency. Hardship Leave helps keep members connected to income and community support.

What management's proposal would do

Management has proposed eliminating Hardship Leave. Our union is pushing back because other leave programs do not replace every contract safety net workers have won together.

What our union is fighting for

  • Keep Hardship Leave available for workers facing serious need.
  • Protect the right of coworkers to support each other.
  • Defend benefits that cost the institution little but matter deeply to workers.
