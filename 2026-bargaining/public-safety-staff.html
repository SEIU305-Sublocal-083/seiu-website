For Campus-Safety Colleagues

Campus safety and campus stability both depend on workers being respected.

This page is for public-safety staff, campus-safety colleagues, and supporters who want to stand with classified staff while speaking carefully for themselves. The June 30 rally is about fair wages, healthcare, staffing stability, and a university that values the work behind daily operations.

Why this connects to campus safety

Campus safety is not only emergency response. It includes reliable facilities, working systems, clean spaces, responsive departments, retention of experienced employees, and predictable processes when something goes wrong. Classified staff support those conditions across OSU every day.

When wages stagnate, healthcare costs rise, and job protections weaken, the university risks losing experienced workers and adding pressure to already busy systems. That affects the whole campus environment.

How to support without speaking for anyone else

  • Attend as an individual supporter if you are able.
  • Use "I support" language unless your group has made a formal endorsement decision.
  • Focus on shared values: stability, retention, safety, accountability, and respect for workers.
  • Share the event link with coworkers who care about campus operations and fair treatment.

Suggested message

"I support classified staff because a stable campus depends on experienced workers in every part of OSU. Fair wages, affordable healthcare, and clear job protections help keep services working for students, staff, faculty, and visitors."
