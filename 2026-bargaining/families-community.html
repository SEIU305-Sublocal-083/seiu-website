For Families and Community Allies

OSU classified staff are part of the community that OSU depends on.

Classified staff are parents, neighbors, alumni, renters, homeowners, volunteers, and local customers. A fair contract keeps experienced workers in public service jobs and helps make OSU a more stable place for students and the surrounding community.

Why this is a community issue

OSU is one of the largest institutions in the region, and its labor choices ripple outward. When classified jobs are stable and fairly paid, workers are more likely to stay in their communities, support local businesses, and keep the university services families count on working smoothly.

When wages stagnate, healthcare costs rise, and protections weaken, the pressure does not stay on campus. It follows workers home and affects families, neighborhoods, and the local economy.

Why families should care

  • Students benefit when experienced staff stay in the jobs that support campus life.
  • Families benefit when public university jobs can support stable lives.
  • Community members benefit when OSU invests in the people who keep the institution functioning.
  • Alumni and neighbors can help make fair treatment a visible public expectation.

How to help

Attend the June 30 rally, invite another community member, and share the event link with a short message about why stable public university jobs matter. You do not need to be an OSU employee to stand with classified staff.
