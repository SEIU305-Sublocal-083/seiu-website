2026 Bargaining Issue Guide

Layoff protections make job cuts less arbitrary and less harmful.

Strong layoff language protects seniority, transparency, and worker rights when management changes staffing levels.

Read the Bargaining HubContact a Steward

Why layoff language matters

Layoff protections are not abstract contract language. They determine whether workers have clear rights, notice, and pathways when a position is reduced or eliminated.

  • Seniority language helps prevent favoritism.
  • Bumping rights can keep experienced workers employed.
  • Clear process gives workers time to seek representation and make informed choices.

What management is trying to weaken

Management's proposal would reduce key layoff protections, including bumping rights. Our union is fighting to keep enforceable language in the contract.

How members can help

Share examples of how layoff uncertainty affects departments, families, and services. Visible member support helps our bargaining team defend rights that matter before a crisis hits.
