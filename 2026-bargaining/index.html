How Bargaining Works

A quick walkthrough of the process from member input to a new contract.

1. Member Priorities

Members identify the issues that matter most through surveys and meetings.

2. Proposal Building

The bargaining team turns priorities into proposals and a strategy.

3. Open Sessions

Bargaining happens at the table with member observers and updates.

4. Strike Vote

Members vote on whether to authorize a strike if needed to win a fair contract.

5. Ratification

Members vote to accept or reject the tentative agreement.

Purple Up on Purple Days

Wear purple to show campus-wide support for a fair contract.

Invite your team to coordinate and snap a photo.

Host a Unity Break

Bring coworkers together for a short break to build unity and share updates.

Host it in your department and invite someone new.

Show Up at Rallies

Bring energy and solidarity to actions that move the table.

Carpool and bring a coworker who hasn’t been yet.

Watch Bargaining

Track how proposals move and share summaries with coworkers.

Sign the Petitions

Add your name and share petitions with coworkers.

Ask a coworker to sign right after you do.

Bring Everyone You Can

Invite coworkers to rallies and actions to show our collective strength.

FAQ

When will bargaining sessions start?

Open bargaining kicks off February 12–13 at UO.

How do I get the Zoom link?

TBD.

How can I participate if I can’t attend live?

We’ll share recaps and action items after each session. You can also connect with a steward to share input.

Do I need to be a member to attend?

All classified staff are encouraged to stay informed. Membership gives you voting rights and a direct voice in decisions.

Where do I get updates and reminders?

We’ll post updates on the News page and email members. You can also email leadership to be added to reminders.

How can I help right now?

Use a bargaining Zoom background, attend sessions when possible, and encourage coworkers to join and participate.
