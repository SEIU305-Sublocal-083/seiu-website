Urgent Bargaining Update

Management is proposing no wage increases for 4 years

No step increases. No COLAs. Higher healthcare costs. Fewer layoff protections.

The universities put a takeaway offer on the table while workers face rising costs. Our union is fighting for real raises, protected benefits, and contract rights we can enforce.

Join the CAT Become a Member Open the Flyer

Right now at the table

They say takeaways. We say fair contract.

Management is trying to freeze wages, shift costs, and weaken protections. Our union is proposing raises, benefits security, and stronger rights for classified staff.

Management's offer

No raises for four years

  • No step increases and no COLAs for four years.
  • Change overtime calculations so workers earn less.
  • Shift healthcare costs onto workers by changing how plans qualify for the 97/3 premium share.
  • Require a doctor's note after 3 consecutive sick days instead of 7.
  • Cap bereavement leave at 8 days per year and let management require documentation of death.
  • Complicate annual vacation cash-out rules.
  • Eliminate layoff protections, including bumping rights during layoffs.
  • Weaken contracting-out protections by removing feasibility studies from the contract.

Our union's proposals

Real raises and real protections

  • July 1, 2026: CPI + 3%, with at least 4% total.
  • July 1, 2027: CPI + 2%, with at least 4% total.
  • Increase the longevity premium by an additional 1% for every 5 years of service.
  • Pilot a 32-hour workweek.
  • Maintain healthcare benefits and add pay-schedule options for PEBB coverage during the academic year.
  • Win union orientations for new employees in their first 30 days.
  • Add job protections and training when work is eliminated by AI, plus transparency around computer monitoring.
  • Strengthen equity, discrimination, adverse-weather, gear, immigrant, and data protections.

What We’re Fighting For

Economics

Raises that beat a four-year wage freeze: CPI-linked increases, step movement, and stronger longevity pay.

Union Power

New employee orientations, stronger communication, and member power at every worksite.

Benefits

Protect affordable healthcare, defend leave, and stop management from shifting more costs onto workers.

Employee Protections

Keep layoff rights, strengthen equity language, protect immigrant workers and data, and address AI-driven job changes.

How Bargaining Works

A quick walkthrough of the process from member input to a new contract.

1. Member Priorities

Members identify the issues that matter most through surveys and meetings.

2. Proposal Building

The bargaining team turns priorities into proposals and a strategy.

3. Open Sessions

Bargaining happens at the table with member observers and updates.

4. Strike Vote

Members vote on whether to authorize a strike if needed to win a fair contract.

5. Ratification

Members vote to accept or reject the tentative agreement.

FAQ

When did bargaining sessions start?

Open bargaining started February 12–13 at UO.

How do I get the Zoom link?

Zoom links are shared by email when observation details are available.

How can I participate if I can’t attend live?

We shared recaps and action items after each session. Members could also connect with a steward to share input.

Do I need to be a member to attend?

All classified staff are encouraged to stay informed. Membership gives you voting rights and a direct voice in decisions.

Where do I get updates and reminders?

We posted updates on the News page and emailed members. Members could also email leadership to be added to reminders.

How can I help right now?

Use a bargaining Zoom background, attend sessions when possible, and encourage coworkers to join and participate.
