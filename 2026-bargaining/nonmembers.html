For Classified Staff

You do not have to be a member yet to be affected by this contract.

If you are classified staff at OSU, bargaining shapes the wages, benefits, and protections around your job. The June 30 rally is a practical first step: stand with coworkers, learn what is at stake, and see what collective power looks like.

Sign Up for the Rally Become a Member

Why this matters if you are not a member

The wages, healthcare costs, layoff language, contracting protections, overtime rules, and leave benefits in the contract shape the job conditions around you. When management proposes no COLAs, no step increases, higher healthcare costs, and weaker protections, those choices affect the whole bargaining unit.

Union membership is how classified staff build enough power to bargain better terms and vote on the contract. The rally is one way to start participating without needing to know every article of the agreement.

What you get by joining

  • A direct voice in union decisions, contract campaigns, and ratification votes.
  • A stronger steward and member network when workplace problems happen.
  • A way to build leverage with coworkers instead of facing management alone.
  • A say in the priorities that shape wages, benefits, safety, workload, and job security.

Reasons to come June 30

You can show management that classified staff are watching the offer closely. You can talk with stewards and coworkers about what the proposals mean in plain language. You can bring questions about membership, the contract, and what happens next.

Simple plan: sign up, wear purple if you can, come to McNary Field from noon to 1 p.m., and bring one coworker who has not been involved yet.
