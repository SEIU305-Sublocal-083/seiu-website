2026 Bargaining Issue Guide

OSU classified staff need COLA raises that keep up with real costs.

A cost-of-living adjustment is not a bonus. It is how wages keep pace when rent, food, transportation, and healthcare costs rise while the work continues.

Read the Bargaining Hub See the 2025 COLA Update

What is at stake

Management's current 2026 bargaining position would freeze COLAs for four years. For classified workers, that means every price increase becomes a pay cut in practice.

  • COLAs protect workers from inflation eating away at base wages.
  • Strong COLAs help OSU recruit and retain the staff who keep the university running.
  • When COLAs disappear, workers absorb rising costs while the institution protects its budget flexibility.

What our union is fighting for

Our union is fighting for economic language that recognizes the actual cost of living for public university workers across Oregon. COLAs work together with step movement, longevity pay, and benefits protection to create a fair wage package.

How members can help

Talk with coworkers about what a four-year wage freeze would mean in your workplace. Wear purple, attend actions, and use the bargaining hub to keep coworkers connected to current updates.
