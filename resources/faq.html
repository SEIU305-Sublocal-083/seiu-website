Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about membership, rights, and how our union works.

What are union dues and how much are they?

Union dues are the way we fund our activities, negotiations, and member support. For SEIU Local 503, dues are 1.7% of your gross monthly salary plus $2.75/month. This is capped at a maximum amount, so you never pay more than the cap regardless of how much you earn. Dues are tax-deductible in some situations, though you should consult a tax professional.

What is the difference between a "member" and a "represented employee"?

All classified staff in our bargaining unit are "represented employees," meaning the contract covers your wages, hours, and working conditions. However, only "members" who have signed a membership card have the right to vote on contracts, vote in union elections, and run for union office. Being a member gives you a voice in the decisions that affect your job.

How do I contact my steward?

You can find a list of stewards on our Stewards page. If you are unsure who to contact or have a general question, you can email the steward council at 083stewards@seiu503.org and a steward will get back to you.

Can I get in trouble for participating in union activities?

No. It is illegal for an employer to retaliate against you for participating in protected union activities, such as attending meetings on your own time, wearing a union button, or filing a grievance. Your rights to organize and advocate for yourself are protected by both state law and our collective bargaining agreement.

When and where are the union meetings?

We hold General Membership Meetings (GMMs) regularly, usually once a month. Check our Events page for the most up-to-date schedule. Meetings are typically held in person on campus with a Zoom option for those who cannot attend in person.
