Free prepared meals

Stone Soup Corvallis says no identification or qualification is required. The city’s posted schedule rotates among:

  • First Congregational UCC, 4515 SW West Hills Dr.
  • First Christian Church, 602 SW Madison Ave.
  • Hygiene Center, 211 SE Chapman Pl.

Call 541-760-6273 or check the city’s current schedule before going.

Safety and crisis help

  • Immediate danger: call 911.
  • CARDV 24-hour crisis line: 541-754-0110.
  • Benton County crisis line: 888-232-7192.
  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: call or text 988.

Domestic-violence shelter locations may be confidential. Call the crisis program rather than traveling to a listed office.

Call, text or browse 211

Dial 211, text your ZIP code to 898211 or use 211info for food, shelter, rent, utilities, health and transportation referrals.

Apply through Oregon ONE

Apply for the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), Oregon Health Plan, cash and child-care benefits online or call the Oregon Department of Human Services at 800-699-9075. Interpreter help is available.

Ask a Community Action Agency

Oregon Housing and Community Services says local agencies may help with energy bills, housing, transportation, employment expenses and other short-term needs.

Food support is for anyone who needs it. Public benefits and charitable programs use their own eligibility rules. Using a pantry says nothing about how you feel about a strike, and Oregon Food Bank’s finder says its sites are open to anyone needing food assistance.

What to bring when you call

Have your ZIP code, household size, immediate need, transportation limits and any shutoff, eviction or benefits notice nearby. Ask about language access, disability access, appointment requirements and documents before traveling.
