Prepare your household for a possible strike.

Use these guides to understand your rights, protect your household and find help before any strike decision. They separate confirmed rules from open questions and welcome members and coworkers who are ready, unsure or skeptical.

Reviewed: July 20, 2026. This page does not announce a strike. Confirm any authorization, notice, start time or instructions through official updates from our union.

Three things to know now

Confirm the call. A lawful Oregon public employee strike follows a process under the Public Employee Collective Bargaining Act, or PECBA.

Limited unemployment insurance may be available. Some eligible striking workers can apply for limited Oregon benefits beginning in 2026.

Asking for help is preparation. Food, housing and benefits programs exist for short-term income loss.

A household-first readiness checklist

Whatever questions you still have about a possible strike, you can take practical steps now to protect your household, records and access to information.

Before any strike

  • Save our union’s contact information outside your work account.
  • Download recent pay statements, leave balances and benefit documents you are allowed to keep.
  • List essential bills and due dates; ask creditors about hardship options early.
  • Review the unemployment insurance and Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) limits before building a budget around them.

If a strike is called

  • Confirm the date, time, covered bargaining unit and instructions through official updates from our union.
  • Use personal devices and accounts for strike activity, and protect confidential work information.
  • If you participate, file for unemployment insurance promptly and keep filing weekly claims during unpaid weeks.
  • Keep copies of management messages and your own factual notes.

During a strike

  • Follow peaceful picket and safety instructions from our strike leaders.
  • Report hours and earnings accurately to benefit programs.
  • Talk with coworkers respectfully. Answer questions with facts, make space for concerns and never use threats or shame.
  • Send media or legal questions to the contacts named by our union.

What these guides can—and cannot—answer

These guides do not announce a strike, tell anyone how to vote, guarantee eligibility for a public benefit or replace advice from our union’s representatives or attorneys. They make the questions understandable and the next steps easier to find.
