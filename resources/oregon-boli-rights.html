Oregon BOLI Worker Rights

BOLI is a key enforcement agency for wage, hour, and workplace protections in Oregon.

Last updated: February 10, 2026

Status: Draft for internal review

Purpose: Member-facing guide, not legal advice

Verification note: Numeric limits and contacts can change; confirm on official links before acting.

What BOLI Covers

BOLI enforces Oregon workplace standards including wage and hour rules, parts of leave law, and civil rights protections. Union workers can have both contract remedies and BOLI remedies depending on the facts.

Key Limits and Numbers (As of February 10, 2026)

  • Civil rights / retaliation filing window: usually 1 year from the harmful act.
  • Final paycheck deadlines: by end of next business day after termination; immediately if employee gives at least 48-hour notice before quitting; otherwise within 5 business days or next regular payday (whichever is first).
  • Late final paycheck penalty: up to 8x regular hourly rate per day, up to 30 days.
  • Standard minimum wage (July 1, 2025 - June 30, 2026): $15.05/hour in standard counties (Benton County).

Time-limit warning: filing windows vary by claim type. Contact your steward and BOLI quickly when an issue starts.

Common Issues for Union Members

  • Unpaid wages, missed overtime, or final paycheck problems.
  • Discrimination, harassment, and retaliation.
  • Leave-related disputes under state protections.
  • Employer policy conflicts with labor standards.

Before You File Anywhere

  1. Contact your steward first.
  2. Gather pay stubs, schedules, time records, and emails.
  3. Document dates, names, and witness information.
  4. Preserve records in one timeline document.
  5. Track both grievance and agency deadlines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I go to BOLI first or my steward first? +

Start with your steward first so contract rights and grievance timelines are protected while you evaluate BOLI options.

Can I pursue a grievance and a BOLI complaint? +

Sometimes yes. The processes differ, so coordinate strategy with your union representative.

What records make the strongest complaint? +

Pay records, schedules, written directives, disciplinary documents, and timeline notes with exact dates and witnesses.
